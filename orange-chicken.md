# Orange Chicken
### Ingredients
- 1 Cup OJ (can be squeezed from oranges)
- 1/4 cup white vinegar
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 4 cloves finely minced garlic
- equal volume of ginger as garlic (no skin)
- salt, pepper, red pepper flakes to taste
- 1-2 tbsp lemon juice if you have it (not necessary)
- ~3tbsp cornstarch
- bucket of flour
- 3-4 eggs
- chicken
- oil for frying
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 1/4 cup honey

### Instructions (Assume about an hour to make everything - frying just takes time, and this is a 2-3 part recipe)
1. fill a pot large enough to fry stuff in, with enough oil to fry stuff in. heat it up until when you run your hand under the faucet and then flick some of the water off your hand into the pot of water, it instantly makes a healthy curdling sound. once the oil is hot, just keep it about that temperature
2. start making some white rice in a separate pot, too
3. chicken breading and frying
    1. while the oil is heating, cut your chicken into pices the size that you would like them to be. pieces about the size of a lumberjack's thumb seem to work pretty well. put them in a bowl on their own **this is bowl 1**. crack enough egg whites into the bowl so that each chicken piece can get completely covered with a sufficient amount of egg white slime. **important: you want only the egg whites, not the yolks!!!**
    2. add 4-5 parts flour, 1 part cornstartch mixture to a separate bowl. you'll want at least 3 cups of flour/cornstartch mixture to start with for breading. **this is bowl 2**
    3. then, take 5-7 pieces of chicken from bowl 1, add them to bowl 2, and work the chicken around in the mixture until it's sufficiently breaded (you want it to be completely covered in the flour/cornstarch mixture)
    4. drop those 5-7 pieces into the pot of hot oil
    5. check the chicken periodically until it's cooked all the way through. once it's cooked, take note of the color of the breading of the cooked chicken. you can use this to more easily guage what cooked chicken looks like in subsequent batches
4. while your comp is cutting the chicken, you can start the sauce:
    1. in a pan, add the orange juice, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, salt, pepper, lemon juice, brown sugar, and honey **not the cornstartch yet!!**
    2. as it heats up, give it a few taste tests once everything is fully dissolved. add extra ingredients as you see fit.
    3. once all of that has been brought to a boil and you're satisfied with the flavor (note that the flavor gets kinda locked in after this step), you can add cornstarch and turn it into a sauce:
        1. mix equal parts **cold water (IT HAS TO BE COLD)** and cornstartch (~3tbsp) in a cup (using a fork helps)
        2. add the cornstartch mix to the boiling sauce base, and stir vigorously until it all thickens up and turns into sauce. I would start by adding 2/3 of the mix, and keep adding more until you get the desired thickness. if you still want it thicker after 3tbsp, mix more cornstarch with cold water and add it
5. after all of the chicken is fried and you're satisfied with your sauce, dump all of the chicken into your sauce pan and mix it around until all the chicken is covered in sauce. if you feel like htere's too much chicken and not enough sauce, cut your losses. better to have some chicken well sauced than a lot of chicken poorly sauced. serve over white rice
